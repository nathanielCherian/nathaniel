- Projects are fun, but are subject to the 80/20 rule. I enjoy building the first 80% of the project. The real meat of it. But I dread the remaining 20% (polishing frontend, fixing random edge cases). Attention to detail is essential for builders, but sometimes I want to make something interesting without the 20% overhead.

- This introduces the idea of sketches. I want to be able to try building something quickly, that exercises some skill or scratches an itch, or if I just want to see how I would approach it. This is similar to how an artist has a cheap disposable sketchbook to jot down random ideas without any pressure of effecting their value as an artist.

- Inspired by Mr. Doob's sketches ![s](https://mrdoob.com/).

- Most of my sketches have a short production timeline of 1-2 days. This is intentional. The lifecycle of a sketch usually begins when I wake up with an idea for something. I usually spend time during the day trying to build it. I usually jump into it quickly, with very little planning. Usually by the next morning or evening, the sketch is complte and published to my website.

- Why publish? Part of the reason all of my sketches are in javascript is for presentation. I enjoy being able to take a cool idea or some interesting technical problem and make it interactive.


- I think more people should create "sketches "
